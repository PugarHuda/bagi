Bagi.
A tip jar that
splits itself.

Trustless USDC tip-splitting on Avalanche C-Chain. The split is enforced on-chain — nobody has to trust a payout admin.

Live demo GitHub by @BangDropID
The problem

Tips pay one wallet.
The split is a promise.

TipeeeStream, StreamElements & co. pay one account — then someone is trusted to manually divide it among the team, mods, and collaborators.

Teams, mod squads, and collab channels get burned by this constantly. There's no neutral referee — just trust that the money gets shared fairly.

The solution

Make the split a property
of the address you tip.

  • ✓On-chain enforcement. A creator registers a split in basis points; every tip auto-routes to each recipient. No payout admin, no trust.
  • ✓Tip by handle, not by hex. Bind twitter:name → wallet; a browser extension adds a “Split-tip” button on social profiles.
  • ✓Micro-tips that make sense. Avalanche C-Chain fees are low enough that a $1 tip doesn't cost $1 to route.
How it works

Three calls. No middleman.

01
setSplit
Creator sets recipients + basis-point shares (must sum to 100%, ≤20 recipients).
02
tip
Anyone tips USDC. Contract takes a ≤1% fee and credits each recipient their exact share — last one absorbs rounding, zero dust lost.
03
withdraw
Pull-based payout. One reverting recipient can't brick anyone else's money.

A separate HandleRegistry maps social handles to wallets, so the extension can resolve @name → address and route through their split.
